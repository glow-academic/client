Problem 2 — Tautology, Contradiction, or Neither
3 × 3 pts
For each formula, determine whether it is a tautology, a contradiction, or neither. Do not use truth tables. If it is a tautology/contradiction, prove it using equivalence rules (name the rules). If neither, give a counterexample assignment.
  1. $((p \to q) \wedge (q \to r)) \to (p \to r)$
  2. $\neg\big(p \wedge (q \vee r)\big) \wedge (p \to q)$
  3. $(p \leftrightarrow q) \wedge (q \leftrightarrow \neg p)$
Hint: Common tools include implication elimination ($p\to q \equiv \neg p \vee q$), De Morgan’s laws, distributivity, absorption, and contradiction/tautology identities.
Problem 3 — Satisfiable or Unsatisfiable
3 × 3 pts
For each statement: either (i) show it is unsatisfiable via equivalences (name the equivalences), or (ii) give an explicit satisfying assignment.
  1. $\neg(p \vee s) \wedge (p \vee s)$
  2. $(a \vee b) \wedge (\neg a \vee c) \wedge (\neg b \vee c) \wedge \neg c$
  3. $(x \to y) \wedge (y \to x) \wedge (x \vee y)$
Problem 4 — Predicate Logic Equivalences
3 × 3 pts
State whether each pair of statements is logically equivalent. If yes, justify (briefly but clearly). If not, give a counterexample structure/interpretation.
  1. $\forall x\,(P(x)\wedge Q(x))$ and $\forall x\,P(x)\ \wedge\ \forall x\,Q(x)$
  2. $\exists x\,(P(x)\vee Q(x))$ and $\exists x\,P(x)\ \vee\ \exists x\,Q(x)$
  3. $\forall x\,\exists y\,R(x,y)$ and $\exists y\,\forall x\,R(x,y)$
Tip: Watch quantifier order carefully. Swapping $\forall$ and $\exists$ is usually not valid.
Problem 5 — Formalizing an English Statement
2 × 4 pts
Universe: all integers. Let $N(x)$ mean “$x$ is negative” and $G(x)$ mean “$x$ is greater than $5$.” Consider the English statement:
“No negative integer is greater than 5.”
For each formula below, decide whether it correctly expresses the statement, and justify.
  1. $\neg\exists x\,\big(N(x)\wedge G(x)\big)$
  2. $\forall x\,\big(N(x)\to \neg G(x)\big)$
Your justification can be short: a 1–3 sentence logical explanation or a concrete counterexample.
Problem 6 — Push Negations Inward
14 pts
Rewrite the following formula so that every negation symbol ($\neg$) appears immediately in front of a predicate (i.e., no negation should apply to a compound formula or quantifier). Show your steps.
$\neg\exists x\,\forall y\,\exists z\ \Big( A(x,y)\ \vee\ \neg B(y,z)\ \wedge\ C(z)\Big)$
Note: Use quantifier-negation rules (e.g., $\neg\forall x\,\phi \equiv \exists x\,\neg\phi$), De Morgan’s laws, and remember $\wedge$ binds tighter than $\vee$.
