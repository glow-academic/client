Assignment Summary:
Given a known NP-complete problem A, reduce it to target problem B to show B is NP-hard.

Examples:
1. 3SAT ≤p CLIQUE
2. VERTEX-COVER ≤p SET-COVER
3. SUBSET-SUM ≤p KNAPSACK

Steps:
- Understand both problem structures
- Define transformation logic between instances
- Prove transformation is polynomial-time
- Explain that a solver for B implies a solver for A

Advice:
- Keep the reduction constructive and readable
- Draw mappings (graph to graph, set to set, etc.)
- Look at Cook-Levin or Karp reductions for patterns
