Statistical Methods Midterm Examination

Question 1 (20 points)

Define and explain the following concepts:

a) Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)

b) Method of Moments

c) Asymptotic efficiency

d) Cramér-Rao Lower Bound

Question 2 (25 points)

Let X₁, X₂, ..., Xₙ be a random sample from a distribution with probability density function:

f(x; θ) = θx^(θ-1), 0 < x < 1, θ > 0

a) Find the method of moments estimator for θ.

b) Find the maximum likelihood estimator for θ.

c) Show that the MLE is consistent.

Question 3 (25 points)

Consider testing H₀: μ = μ₀ versus H₁: μ ≠ μ₀ for a normal distribution with unknown variance.

a) Derive the likelihood ratio test statistic.

b) Show that this test is equivalent to a two-tailed t-test.

c) Discuss the relationship between confidence intervals and hypothesis tests in this context.

Question 4 (20 points)

Explain the concept of sufficiency and provide an example. State and use the Factorization Theorem to show that a statistic is sufficient for a parameter.

Question 5 (10 points)

What is the difference between a consistent estimator and an unbiased estimator? Can an estimator be consistent but biased? Provide an example.

Formula Sheet Reference:

• Likelihood Function: L(θ) = ∏ᵢ f(xᵢ; θ)
• MLE: θ̂ maximizes L(θ) or log L(θ)
• Cramér-Rao Bound: Var(θ̂) ≥ 1/I(θ), where I(θ) is Fisher Information
• Factorization Theorem: T is sufficient if f(x;θ) = g(T(x);θ)h(x)
